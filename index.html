Our Story

I'm Weldon Rono, a tea farmer in Buteti, I have seen the untapped brilliance of our youth. They possess the ambition buy lack the digital tools to complete in a globalized economy.

Nexus was born in the AIC Chemwonjo Church hall—not as a corporate initiative, but as a neighborly promise. We believe that technology should serve our culture, not replace it.

Our Mission

To equip rural youth in Bureti with future‑ready digital skills, real‑world projects, and a pathway to dignified work – without leaving their community or their culture behind.

Our Values

🌱 Rooted – technology serves our way of life 🛠️ Hands‑on – build something real every session 👥 Peer‑led – youth make the decisions

Frequently Asked Questions

📌 Who can join Nexus? +
Youth aged 14–24 from Bureti, especially tea‑farming households. No prior experience needed. Applications open in early 2027.
💰 Is there a cost? +
No. The program is 100% free for participants, thanks to grants and community support.
⏳ How long is the program? +
6 months (24 sessions, 2 hours per week), starting April 2027.
🎓 Do I get a certificate? +
Yes – a “Certificate of Digital Mastery” at the graduation Expo.
🖥️ Can I join if I have no laptop? +
Yes – shared laptops will be provided during sessions.
📝 How do I apply? +
Application details will be posted here before April 2027. For now, contact us to express interest.
